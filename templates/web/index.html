ATMOSPHRIC OPTIC'S GROUP OF CAMAGUEY

"GOAC's main goal is The study of radiative transfer processes in the atmosphere in the conditions of our country"

Research Areas

Solar Radiation

The GOAC team Works in fitting the numerical models simulating the processes of radiative transfer of the solar radiation in the earth’s atmosphere to the local conditions existing in Camagüey and Cuba. We also conducted the data rescue of the more than 40 years series of solar radiation measurements of the Estación Actinométrica de Camagüey and we are engaged in the rescue of the long series of measurements from the other three actinometrical stations of the country. Resulting from the continuation of the data rescue of the current observations we have implemented the Servicio de Diagnóstico de la Radiación Solar en Camagüey, which we also envisage to be extended to rest of the actinometrical station of the country.

Aerosols

During the last decade GOAC has been measuring optical properties of aerosols using a state of the art sun photometer, contributing to RIMA and AERONET. We also use broadband aerosols optical depth (BAOD), derived from the direct radiation measurements at the actinometrical stations in the country to characterize long-term trends of aerosols. We characterized the local aerosols types and conducted case studies. Particular effort will be dedicated to characterize the Saharan dust reaching Cuba after its transport across the Atlantic.

Clouds

Clouds play the main role among all the atmospheric components in the regulation of the amount of solar radiation reaching the earth surface. GOAC conducted studies on cirrus clouds features in our region using measurements from ground based lidar and satellite instruments. Its radiative impact has been also studied. Future research to quantify the radiative forcing of other types of clouds is envisaged.

ABOUT US

The Atmospheric Optics Group of Camagüey (GOAC from its Spanish name) has its origins in the research group initially formed to study stratospheric aerosols, the Camagüey Lidar Station (CLS), from which took its original name. The natural development of the group is part of the working strategy designed in the early 90’s of last century. That strategy was oriented to the assimilation of knowledge and know-how for conducting research on solar radiative transfer processes in the physical geographical conditions of our country and region. Its execution has enabled the group to continue the research on solar radiation transfer processes while it is beginning to provide scientific and technical services of high added value.
